### Core Premise

> **You are not ghosted. You are not indexed.**

LinkedIn SEO = optimizing your profile for **indexing, search, and discovery**.

Google indexes websites. LinkedIn indexes people.

SEO means making the system understand:
- who you are
- what you do
- when to show you

You have to be **visible** before you can be **chosen**.

---

### The Index: Google SEO vs LinkedIn SEO

Google indexes pages. LinkedIn indexes people. Same architecture:
`documents → indexed fields → query → ranked results`

| Google SEO | LinkedIn SEO |
|---|---|
| web page | your profile |
| `<title>` tag | headline (220 chars) |
| meta description | first 3 lines of About |
| H1 / content | job titles + experience text |
| tags / categories | Skills section (up to 100) |
| URL | custom profile URL (`/in/your-name`) |

> **If a word is not in these fields — for the search engine it does not exist in you.**

### The Recruiter's Hiring Map

Most students prepare for interviews. Many disappear **before** interviews.

```
1. JOB REQUISITION → 2. SEARCH PROCESS → 3. PROFILE SCREENING → 4. INTERVIEW → 5. OFFER
```

**Today we only care about steps 1–3: where invisibility happens.**

### The Other Side of the Screen

Recruiters don't scroll. They query.

**40+ Search Filters:**
- Job title (current / past)
- Skills — exact checkbox match
- Location / open to relocation
- Years of experience / seniority
- Companies, schools, industry
- Spoken languages

**Priority "Spotlights":**
- **Open to Work** — shown as a separate candidate pool
- **"More likely to respond"** — based on your activity
- **"Engaged with your brand"** — followed / interacted

Every spotlight is a switch **you** control from a free account.

> **No filter match = you are not in the result set. Not rejected — just absent.**

### Step 1 — Job Requisition (Internal API Spec)

Recruiters do not start with your personality. They start with a brief.

```json
{
  "role_title": "Junior Frontend Developer",
  "must_have": ["React", "JavaScript", "Git"],
  "nice_to_have": ["TypeScript", "REST API"],
  "location": ["Tashkent", "Remote"],
  "level": "Junior / Intern"
}
```

A job requisition defines:
- Why the role exists
- Skills + tools required
- Level / seniority
- Budget / timeline
- What "matching" means

> **If this spec is not visible in your profile, you are outside the shortlist.**

---

### Query Anatomy: Boolean Search — The Recruiter's Syntax

```
("Software engineer" OR "Graphic designer" OR "Digital Marketing")
AND (Instagram OR "Meta Ads" OR TikTok)
AND (Tashkent OR Uzbekistan) NOT (intern OR student)
```

| Operator | Function | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| `" "` | Exact phrase | "SMM Manager" matches only that exact phrase — word forms matter |
| `AND / OR` | Narrow / widen | OR-groups = synonyms. You must contain at least one word from **every** group |
| `NOT` | Exclusion | The word "student" in your headline can **exclude** you from real searches |

### Profile Surgery: Bad vs Searchable

Passion is not searchable. Tools, roles, industries, and proof are searchable.

**❌ BAD:**
```
Student | Junior Developer | Open to Work
I love coding and learning new things.
```

**✅ SEARCHABLE:**
```
Junior Frontend Developer | React · JavaScript · TypeScript | REST API · Git | Tashkent

Junior Frontend Developer building responsive web apps with React, JavaScript,
TypeScript and REST APIs. Interested in fintech, SaaS and product teams.
```

### The Additional Source: business.linkedin.com

The exact place recruiters take their job descriptions from. Open. Free. Nobody reads it as a candidate.

**Path:**
`business.linkedin.com → Resources & Support → Job Description Templates → 380+ roles A–Z`

**What each section gives you:**

| Section | Use |
|---|---|
| **Objectives Block** | The role's purpose in the company's words — this language goes into your About |
| **Responsibilities** | The literal verbs and nouns of the vacancy: "digital strategy", "ROI", "stakeholders" — your experience bullets should speak this dialect |
| **Skills Lists** | Required-skills sections map 1:1 to your Skills checkboxes — copy the **exact spelling** |
| **Interview Questions** | Same library ships interview questions per role — you see the exam before taking it |

> **Knowing the system by heart = read their playbook, write your profile in it.**

### The 3 Major Pillars of Your LinkedIn Presence

```
1. PROFILE
2. CONNECTIONS
3. UPDATES
```

---

### PILLAR 1 — PROFILE

> **Build once. Get discovered forever.**

Utilize all profile sections if possible.

**Must-Haves:**

- 📸 **Clear photo** — trust = clicks
- 🧠 **Headline: value, not job title**
  - ❌ "Student"
  - ✅ "Future Data Analyst | Turning Data into Insights"
- ✍️ **About section:** story + direction
- 🧩 **Add to profile:**
  - Education
  - Skills
  - Projects
  - Experience (even small)

> **Rule:** If someone sees your profile for 5 seconds — do they understand who you are?

---

### PILLAR 2 — CONNECTIONS

> **Your network is your net worth.**

**How to build:**
- Send & accept requests
- Add 5–10 relevant people **daily**
- Always include a short message:
  > *"Saw your work in X — would love to connect"*
- Engage with their content **before** asking to connect

**Formula:**
```
Comment → Connect → Build relationship → Opportunities
```

---

### PILLAR 3 — UPDATES

> **Consistency beats everything.**

#### 6 Types of LinkedIn Content You Should Create

| # | Type | Purpose | Benefits | Example |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 01 | **Educational Posts** | Teach something valuable in your niche | Builds authority, positions you as an expert | "3 copywriting mistakes killing response rate" |
| 02 | **Personal Stories** | Share real experiences & lessons | Builds trust, emotional connection | "3 years ago I had 700 followers…" |
| 03 | **Step-by-Step** | Break down processes into simple steps | High saves, dwell time, algorithm loves it | "How to write a post in 15 minutes" |
| 04 | **Contrarian Takes** | Challenge popular opinions | Sparks debate, makes you memorable | "Unpopular opinion: Cold Outreach is dead" |
| 05 | **Behind-the-Scenes** | Show the real, unpolished side | Humanizes your brand, builds relatability | "My content planning process" |
| 06 | **Lists** | Deliver quick, scannable value | Easy to consume, highly shareable | "7 free AI tools for content creation" |

#### Consistency Strategy

- **2–3 posts per week** > 10 posts once
- **15 minutes daily:** Comment → Reply → Connect
- Conversations > Likes
- Comments = gold

#### Content Tactics

- Replies boost reach even more
- Write posts that **invite responses**

#### Content Types (What to Post About)

- What you're **learning**
- What you're **building**
- What you're **thinking**
